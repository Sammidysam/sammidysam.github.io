Current Location Teaching bass is best done in-person and not online, so for now I am based in (and can teach students in) the Chicagoland area. I will add, though, that I am lenient on what I define as Chicagoland. If I can form a partnership with a community that is even up to a two-hour drive away, I could be interested in coming by once per week.

Contact Me I am interested in teaching bass if it interests you! The easiest way to get in touch is to email me at craigsamm@gmail.com.

Rates, etc. It feels wrong to have an advertised rate on a website like this, as it will always change depending on my personal circumstances and my current point in my teaching journey. I will say that I never want the rate to be too steep to prevent bass study as an option, though. I intend to be working in software engineering, so I expect that my rates will be flexible. We can be in touch.

Philosophy My biggest goal when teaching is for the material to be fun. If you love a song or a music group, let's explore that music and learn how it works and how we can play it! I happened to fall in love with classical music in the end, but it is not a requirement for you to. I am happy for us to chase your passion, together. In more direct terms, I expect lessons to cover technique (how can we play in a way that will never hurt and allow you to play into old age?), harmony (scales, chords, etc.), and melody (playing songs that you love).

Past Experience I have been thrilled to get the opportunity to teach both in high school and at Oberlin Conservatory. In high school, I started teaching bass during my junior year, and proceeded to have four students total over the next two years. At Oberlin, I have volunteered with the public school orchestra a fair amount. During my first year, I helped out Oberlin's high school orchestra bassists, then their middle school orchestra bassists during my second year. During my third year, I started teaching some bass students in the Northern Ohio Youth Orchestra (NOYO) privately. I also taught private lessons to some students in the Oberlin public school system through Oberlin Conservatory's String Pedagogy II class. During the Covid pandemic, I taught one family friend online. When vaccinations started being abundant, I taught two students in NOYO in-person again.
